This MUTUAL NON -DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT (this “ Agreement ”) is entered into as of June  16, 202 5 by and 
between Fanuc America Corporation, 3900 West Hamlin Road, Rochester Hills, MI 48309 -3253, USA  and Kidde -
Fenwal, LLC, 400 Main Street, Ashland, MA 01721, USA . 
 
WHEREAS, the parties are evaluating the possibility of entering into a mutually beneficial business 
arrangement  regarding proprietary data ; and   
 
WHEREAS, each party, in the course of such discussions, may consider it appropriate to disclose 
confidential information to the other.  
 
NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants set forth below and other good 
and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, and intending 
to be legally bound, the parties agree as follows:  
 
1. Definition .  For purposes of this Agreement, “ Confidential Information ” shall mean any and all informa tion, 
documents and know -how owned, possessed or used by one party hereto (the “ Discloser ”) that the Discloser 
discloses or has disclosed to the other party (the “ Recipient ”) or to which the Recipient gains or has gained 
access by virtue of the parties’ relationship, which either (1) which is noted and clearly labeled confidential, 
or if transmitted visually or orally is subsequently reduced to writing and clearly labeled confidential within 
fifteen (15) days of disclosure , or (2) that the other party knows or should reasonably know to be of a sensitive 
or proprietary nature, including, without limitation, any proposal, marketing or business plan, vendor 
information, customer information, financial data and personnel data; provided , that Confidential Information 
shall not include information which (a) is or becomes available to the public other than as a result of disclosure 
by the Recipient or its employees in violation of this Agreement; (b) was known to the Recipient prior to 
Recipient’s receiving the same pursuant to this Agreement and not otherwise restricted by contract or law; 
(c) becomes available to the Recipient on a non -confidential basis from a third person or source not restricted 
by contract or law regarding such information , or (d) was independently developed.  
 
2. Protection of Confidential Information .  The Recipient will protect the confidentiality of Confidential 
Information with the same degree of care as the Recipient uses for its own Confidential Information, but no 
less than a reasonable degree of care.   
 
3. Use.  The Recipient shall use the Discloser’s Confidential Information solely for the evaluation purposes 
contemplated by this Agreement, and for no other purposes whatsoever.  
 
4. Non -Disclosure .  The Recipient, without the Discloser’s prior written consent, shall not disclose the Discloser’s 
Confidential Information to any third party; provided , however, that any of such information may be disclosed 
to (i) employees of the Recipient who need to know such information for the purposes of evaluating the 
possibility of entering into a contract or contracts with the Discloser (it being understood tha t the Recipient 
shall remain fully liable for any and all breaches by any such employees of the confide ntiality obligations 
hereunder) and (ii) those parties to whom the Recipient is required by law, regulatory agency or judicial or 
other governmental body to disclose such information.  Furthermore, neither party shall, without the other 
party’s written con sent, disclose to any third party the fact that discussions are taking or have taken place 
concerning the potential business relationship contemplated hereby or other facts with respect to such 
discussions, including any terms, conditions or the status the reof, or the fact that any Confidential Information 
has been made available hereunder, and neither party shall otherwise make any public disclosure with respect 
to this Agreement or the matters contemplated hereby, except as required by law, regulatory age ncy or 
judicial or other governmental body.

Page 2 of 3 5. Destruction of Materials .  Upon written request by the Discloser, the Recipient shall destroy all written 
material in any form whatsoever which contains the Discloser’s Confidential Information, including all internal 
notes, memoranda, and all copies, extracts or other reproducti ons thereof.  Notwithstanding the foregoing, 
the Recipient shall be permitted to retain physical and electronic copies of any materials containing the 
Discloser’s Confidential Information solely to the minimum extent necessary to co mply with applicable laws, 
regulations and bona fide internal document retention policies.  
 
6. No Obligation .  Both parties agree that, unless and until a definitive agreement with respect to any 
contemplated transaction between the parties has been executed and delivered, neither the Disclosing Party 
nor the Recipient will be under any legal obligation of any k ind whatsoever with respect to the transaction, 
except for the matters specifically agreed to herein.   
 
7. Injunctive Relief .  The parties expressly acknowledge that damages alone will be an inadequate remedy for 
any breach or violation of the provisions of this Agreement in view of the difficulties of placing a monetary 
value on the Confidential Information, and each party sha ll be entitled to a preliminary and final injunction, 
without bond, to prevent any breach or further breach of this Agreement or further unauthorized use of 
Confidential Information.  This remedy is separate and apart from any other remedy  such party may have at 
law or in equity.  
 
8. Term .  This Agreement  and the confidentiality obligations herein shall automatically terminate on the date 
which is three  (3) year s after the date hereof, provided, however, that any obligation of confidentiality with 
respect to any information that qualifies as a trade secret under the requirements of any applicable law will 
survive as long as that information is classified as such.   
 
9. Miscellaneous .  This Agreement shall be binding on the parties and their respective affiliates, subsidiaries, 
successors and assigns. This Agreement constitutes the complete agreement between the parties and 
supersedes all prior agreements, oral or written, and any oth er communication relating to the subject matter 
of this Agreement.  This Agreement may not be amended or modified except in a writing executed by both 
parties and will be governed by the laws of the State of California excluding conflicts of l aw principles.  If any 
provision or portion of any provision of this Agreement shall be determined to be void, invalid or 
unenforceable for any reason, the validity and enforceability of the remaining provisions or portions of 
provisions will not be affect ed.  
 
10. Counterparts .  This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

representatives, on the date and year first above written.
